Challenges in Traditional Veterinary Communication

There are many inherent communication challenges in the traditional veterinary communication systems, affecting the overall operational efficiency of veterinary clinics and the satisfaction level of the pet parents.

For instance, based on a study by Pet Desk, the most common challenges to traditional veterinary communication systems include:

  • 50% - lack of available slots
  • 35% - long phone hold times
  • 33% - unable to book an appointment when the clinic’s closed
  • 24% - inability to book online
  • 21% - difficulty contacting the clinic
  • 17% - complicated booking process

Oftentimes, the primary hindrance is limited clinic hours, restricting timely communication. Due to manual workforce dependency, the operating hours are limited, delaying client interaction outside regular hours. For urgent concerns outside regular hours, it can even lead to pet parents being unable to receive a prompt diagnosis, causing anxiety, delayed treatments, or even health deterioration of the animal in some cases.

Another dominant cause of disruptive communication in traditional vet clinics is high call volumes leading to missed inquiries, appointments, and delayed responses.

Do Administrative burdens on Staff affect client satisfaction?

Beyond these two, the sheer number of administrative burdens on staff highly affects the quality of client interactions, ultimately leading to a bad client satisfaction. Receptionists often continuously switch between appointment scheduling, medical record management, and follow-ups while handling the high influx of incoming calls. As a norm for every industry, human resources are limited. Similarly, a lack of human resources leads to an overall decreased operational efficiency in vet clinics.

How Does Dependency on a Manual Work Force Cause Communication Issues?

Additionally, not having a centralized feature-rich communication channel causes veterinary teams to heavily depend on phone calls, voicemails, and manual record-keeping. This further adds up to increased chances of errors, miscommunication, and client dissatisfaction. This significant manual workforce dependency is a prime root cause for many of these communication issues.

Thus, there is a huge market necessity for a digitally powered communication system, such as inbound Voice AI in veterinary practices, to revolutionize traditional veterinary communications.
