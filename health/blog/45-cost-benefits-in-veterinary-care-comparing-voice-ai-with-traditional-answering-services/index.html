Traditional Veterinary Answering Services: An Overview

Veterinary clinics that are still using traditional answering services offer 24/7 call management, proficiently handle scheduling of appointments, and initial emergency triage based on already set protocols. In these answering services, human operators make sure that pet owners get tailored support at any hour.

What is the Cost Structure in Traditional Answering Services?

The pricing model for these services involves fixed fees that need to be paid monthly. This provides you with a set number of call handling minutes or per-minute charges that consistently change based on the volume of calls. But, if you apply for specialized services or exceed the specified number of minutes, then you need to be prepared to pay extra charges

What are the Pros and Cons of Traditional Answering Services?

Despite being old school, the human touch offers instant reassurance and empathy to pet owners that their pet will soon be fine. However, these services have higher costs when you have to operate them, increasing the risk of human error while taking messages or scheduling appointments, and may experience limitations in adjusting when needed during rush hours.
