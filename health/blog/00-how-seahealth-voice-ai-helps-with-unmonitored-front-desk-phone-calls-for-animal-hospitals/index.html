How Does It Work?

The SeaHealth Voice AI system integrates with the existing telephony infrastructure of a veterinary clinic to ensure efficient call handling. Below is a step-by-step breakdown of how calls are processed through different components in the system.

Step-by-step process for SeaHealth voice AI call handling:

1. Incoming call via Phone Jack (Analog/VoIP)

The call originates from a customerâ€™s phone and reaches the clinic through an analog or VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol) system. If using analog, the call passes through a traditional phone line. If using VoIP, the call travels through an internet-based phone system.

2. Connection to Modem/Router

The modem/router acts as a gateway between the phone system and the internet. For VoIP calls, the router ensures smooth data transmission over an IP network.

3. A recorder captures the call

A call recorder (if installed) logs the conversation for quality control, compliance, and analysis. The system ensures that sensitive customer information is handled securely.

4. A voice AI system processes the call

The AI system receives the call and uses Natural Language Processing (NLP) to analyze the callerâ€™s speech. The AI detects intent, such as appointment booking, medical inquiries, or emergency cases. It also assesses sentiment and tone, determining if the caller is stressed, angry, or calm.

5. Voice AI interacts with the caller

The AI provides pre-programmed responses based on the caller’s inquiry. If the issue is routine (e.g., clinic hours, services), the AI answers directly. If urgent, the AI escalates the call to a human receptionist or veterinarian.

6. Dashboard updates in real time

The AI logs call data into a dashboard, which provides:

Call analytics (number of calls, missed calls, and response time)

Caller sentiment analysis (tone detection: calm, frustrated, emergency)

Appointment booking status

Medical vs. non-medical inquiries

7. Call routing and escalation

If the call needs human intervention, the AI system redirects it back to the front desk or veterinarian through the phone jack (analog or VoIP). The receptionist gets an alert with relevant caller details before answering.
